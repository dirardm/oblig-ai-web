ObligaI is a regulatory obligation management platform for financial institutions operating across multiple jurisdictions. It translates regulatory texts into structured obligations and maps them to the procedures that satisfy them — making the regulatory perimeter of an institution visible, auditable, and maintainable.

Overview

What ObligaI does.

The Challenge

Financial institutions operating across jurisdictions carry hundreds of regulatory obligations. Most are managed in spreadsheets. Few are connected to the procedures that satisfy them. Fewer still are monitored for conflicts between regulators.

The Platform

Six layers from regulatory source to compliance workbench. Each obligation has a WHAT — the legal requirement, read-only — and a HOW — the institution's documented procedure for satisfying it. Coverage is the percentage of obligations with a documented HOW.

The Architecture

Conflict detection is a structural property of the knowledge graph. When two regulations give incompatible instructions on the same data activity, the graph surfaces it as a conflict node — not as a keyword match, not as a manual review flag.

Platform Architecture

Six layers from regulator to workbench.

01
Client Profile
Legal entity, business lines, international footprint, risk profile. Determines which regulators apply.
↓
02
Regulatory Sources
Supervisory authorities with jurisdiction over the institution. Scope defined by client profile.
↓
03
Regulatory Library
Regulatory frameworks mapped to the institution's perimeter. Full-text ingestion and NLP extraction in Phase 2.
↓
04
Knowledge Graph
Ontological mapping of regulators, regulations, obligations and conflicts. Structural conflict detection.
↓
05
Obligation Register
Each obligation: a WHAT (the legal requirement, read-only) and a HOW (the institution's documented procedure). Coverage = percentage of obligations with a documented HOW.
↓
06
Compliance Workbench
The analyst interface. Obligation detail, evidence locker, action panel, audit trail. Every action logged with timestamp and actor.

How it works

Three ideas worth understanding.

Six layers from regulatory source to compliance workbench. Layer 05 is where compliance work happens.

One platform. Two completely different regulatory worlds. The platform adapts to the institution — not the other way around.

When two regulations give incompatible instructions on the same activity, ObligaI surfaces the conflict as a node in the knowledge graph — automatically, structurally.
