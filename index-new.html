The regulatory obligation challenges.

Financial institutions operating across jurisdictions in different geographies face a tremendous amount of regulatory requirements — distributed across multiple instruments, regulatory levels, and supervisory authorities. The complete picture is assembled manually, over days, by experience. Requirements discovered late — at validation, audit, or supervisory review — cost significantly more to address than requirements identified at the outset.

The regulatory perimeter of an institution is not a flat list. It is a graph.

Six layers from regulatory source
to obligations mapped to policies and procedures.

01

Client Profile

Legal entity, business lines, international regulatory footprint, risk profile, products, markets, geographies, extraterritorial coverage. These determine the scope of every subsequent layer.

02

Regulatory Sources

Supervisory authorities with jurisdiction over the institution. Scope defined by client profile.

03

Regulatory Library

Regulatory instruments ingested from primary sources and structured at (sub)article level. Each article classified by its regulatory function.

04

Knowledge Graph

Requirements linked across instruments, regulatory levels, and jurisdictions. Cross-instrument references resolved automatically. Tension and/or incompatibility detection is structural.

05

Obligation Register

WHAT — the regulatory requirement, read-only with full source provenance. HOW — the institution's documented procedure for compliance.

06

Compliance Workbench

Role-based prefilled regulatory checklists. Each function sees only the regulations relevant to its work. All output is advisory.

What makes it different.

Complete regulatory picture

All requirements bearing on a matter — traversed across instruments and levels in a single pass. No requirement is missed because a source was not manually identified.

Advisory output

ObligaI surfaces what regulations say and presents candidate requirements for the institution to review and adopt. Compliance determinations remain with the institution.

Full provenance

Every checklist item traces to a specific article in a specific instrument at a specific level of the regulatory hierarchy. The audit trail is built in.
